How Much Does It Cost to Paint the Exterior of a House in the UK? 2026 Guide

Painting the exterior of your house is one of the most impactful home improvements you can make, but understanding the true cost is essential for budgeting. In 2026, exterior painting prices in the UK vary significantly depending on your property size, condition, and location. This guide breaks down what you can expect to pay and the factors that influence your final quote.

Average Exterior Painting Costs in the UK

For a typical three-bedroom semi-detached house in the UK, exterior painting costs generally range from GBP1,500 to GBP3,500. A four-bedroom detached property might cost between GBP2,500 and GBP5,000, depending on the amount of preparation work needed and the condition of existing paintwork. Labour typically accounts for 60-70% of the total cost, with materials making up the remainder.

The national average labour rate for exterior painters in 2026 sits around GBP25-45 per hour, though this varies by region. In Manchester and Lancashire, where The Final Layer operates, you'll often find competitive pricing compared to London and the South East. If your property requires significant prep work such as sanding, filling cracks, or treating damp, expect costs to increase accordingly.

Factors That Affect Your Exterior Painting Quote

Several key variables influence the final cost of your exterior painting project. The size and height of your property is primary: single-storey bungalows cost less to paint than three-storey Victorian homes. Accessibility matters too; if painters need scaffolding, cherrypickers, or safety equipment, this adds GBP300-800 to your bill. Difficult access around gardens, fences, or neighbouring properties also increases costs.

The current condition of your exterior significantly impacts pricing. Properties with sound, well-maintained paintwork need less preparation, reducing costs. However, if your exterior has peeling paint, wood rot, loose rendering, or mould, painters must spend time preparing surfaces—sometimes doubling labour costs. Weather exposure also plays a role; north-facing walls that retain moisture may require specialist treatments. Finally, your choice of paint quality matters: premium exterior paints cost more upfront but last longer and provide better protection, potentially saving money over time.

Painting Costs by Property Type in Manchester and Lancashire

Terraced houses in Manchester typically cost GBP1,200-2,500 to paint externally, as they're smaller with straightforward access. Semi-detached properties in Lancashire average GBP1,800-3,200, while detached family homes usually fall between GBP2,800-4,500. Period properties such as Victorian or Edwardian houses often cost more—sometimes GBP3,500-6,000—because their architectural details, ornate features, and rendered walls demand extra care and time.

Bungalows are often the most cost-effective to paint at GBP1,200-2,200 due to their single-storey height and reduced scaffolding requirements. Modern properties with render or cladding typically cost GBP2,000-4,000. Ibrahim Alzoubi at The Final Layer recommends getting three quotes for any exterior job, as costs can vary based on painters' experience and local knowledge of Manchester and Lancashire properties. This ensures you receive fair pricing and understand exactly what preparation work is included.

What's Included in an Exterior Painting Quote?

A transparent quote should itemise labour, materials, scaffolding, waste disposal, and insurance. Labour usually covers surface cleaning, masking, filling cracks, priming, and applying two coats of quality exterior paint. The quote should specify the paint brand and type—exterior gloss, satin, or textured finishes—as cheaper paints may need repainting within 3-5 years rather than 7-10 years with premium brands.

Many quotes exclude items such as replacing broken downpipes, treating timber rot, or repointing brickwork. Ask whether jet washing, gutter cleaning, or repairs to fascias and soffits are included or quoted separately. Insurance and scaffolding setup are sometimes listed separately, and disposal fees for old paint and materials should be outlined. Request a detailed timeline; most exterior jobs take 1-3 weeks depending on property size and weather. Reputable painters like The Final Layer provide written guarantees on their work, typically covering paint failure for 5-10 years.

Money-Saving Tips for Exterior Painting

Consider painting during spring or autumn when demand is lower and painters often offer better rates than summer. Bundle multiple jobs together—painting alongside gutter repairs or fence treatment—and negotiate a package discount. If your exterior is in reasonable condition, focus on repainting rather than stripping everything back, which significantly reduces preparation costs and labour time.

Choose durable mid-range paints rather than the cheapest options; you'll save money by avoiding repainting within a few years. If scaffolding is required, sharing it with a neighbour having simultaneous work can split costs. Obtain multiple quotes and don't automatically choose the cheapest; a skilled painter using quality materials often delivers better value. Finally, regular maintenance between paintings—such as keeping gutters clean and sealing small cracks early—extends the life of your paintwork and reduces long-term costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does exterior house painting typically take?

Most exterior painting projects take 1-3 weeks depending on property size, condition, and weather. A three-bedroom semi-detached house usually takes 7-10 working days. Weather delays can extend timelines, so factor in 10-15% additional time during British weather seasons.

How often should I repaint my house exterior?

Quality exterior paintwork typically lasts 5-10 years in the UK climate. Properties exposed to harsh weather, harsh sunlight, or coastal salt spray may need repainting every 3-5 years. Regular inspection for peeling paint or damage helps you catch problems early.

Do I need planning permission to paint my house exterior?

No, standard exterior painting doesn't require planning permission in most UK properties. However, listed buildings or properties in conservation areas may require consent before changing paint colour dramatically. Check with your local council if you're unsure.

What's the best time of year to paint the exterior in Manchester and Lancashire?

Late spring through early autumn offers the best conditions, as drier weather prevents paint streaking and allows proper drying. Avoid winter months when moisture and cold temperatures compromise paint adhesion. Late March to September is ideal, though mild, dry days in autumn are also suitable.
