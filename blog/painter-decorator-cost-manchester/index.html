How Much Does a Painter and Decorator Cost in Manchester? [2026 Guide]

Planning to repaint a room or your whole house in Manchester but unsure what it should cost? This guide covers typical painter and decorator prices for 2026, what affects the price, and how to make sure you are getting a fair quote.

What Affects the Cost of Painting and Decorating?

Several factors determine how much a painter and decorator will charge in Manchester. The size of the area is the most obvious — a small bedroom takes far less time than a large open-plan living room. Just as important is the condition of the surfaces: rooms with damaged plaster, peeling paint or old wallpaper that needs stripping require significantly more preparation before any colour goes on the walls.

The type of work also matters. Interior rooms are generally less complex than exterior painting, which involves working at height, preparing weathered surfaces and using specialist products designed to withstand the elements. Feature walls and wallpaper hanging take longer per square metre than straightforward emulsion work, and are priced accordingly.

Finally, the specification of materials makes a difference. Premium paints cost more per litre but provide better coverage and durability, reducing the number of coats needed. A decorator who uses quality products may quote slightly more than one using budget materials — but the result will look better and last longer.

Room-by-Room Price Guide for Manchester (2026)

These are typical labour-only costs for painting and decorating in Manchester in 2026. They assume surfaces are in reasonable condition; additional charges apply if filling, sanding or wallpaper stripping is needed before painting can begin.

  • Single bedroom (walls & ceiling): £200–£350 — add £100–£150 for woodwork (skirtings, architrave, door)
  • Living room or dining room (walls & ceiling): £300–£500 — add £150–£250 for full woodwork
  • Full 3-bedroom house repaint (all rooms, hallway, stairs, landing): £1,200–£2,500
  • Exterior semi-detached house: £800–£2,200 depending on size and condition
  • Wallpaper hanging (one feature wall): £150–£300 for labour, depending on paper type

These are guide prices only. Every property is different, and an accurate figure always requires seeing the job first. Prices above are for labour only and exclude the cost of paint and materials unless agreed otherwise.

Day Rate vs Fixed Project Quote

Painter and decorators in Manchester typically charge either a day rate or a fixed project price. Day rates generally run from £180 to £280 per day for a sole trader, depending on experience and the type of work involved. For small or unpredictable jobs, a day rate is straightforward and flexible.

For most residential projects, a fixed project quote is better for the customer. You know the total cost before work starts, there are no surprises if a job takes longer than expected, and the decorator is motivated to work efficiently. It also makes it much easier to compare quotes fairly.

What Should Be Included in the Price?

A professional painter and decorator should include the following as standard: laying dust sheets and protecting furniture and flooring; light filling of minor cracks and imperfections before painting; applying the agreed number of coats with the specified product; and clearing up and leaving the space tidy at the end of each day.

What is typically not included in a standard quote: significant plastering repairs, removing radiators to paint behind them, or supplying specialist materials such as wallpaper. These should be discussed upfront and quoted separately where needed.

Always confirm what is included before accepting a quote. A price that excludes preparation work or uses budget-grade materials often costs more in the long run — either through a poor finish that needs redoing sooner, or unexpected extras added mid-job.

How to Get a Fair Quote in Manchester

The most reliable way to get an accurate quote is an on-site visit. A good decorator needs to see the condition of the surfaces, understand the level of preparation required and hear exactly what you want. Quotes given over the phone without seeing the work are often inaccurate in either direction.

Get two or three quotes from different decorators and compare them carefully. Be cautious of quotes that come in very much lower than the others — it usually means something has been excluded or corners will be cut. The cheapest quote is rarely the best value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a painter and decorator charge per day in Manchester?

Day rates in Manchester typically run from £180 to £280 per day for a sole trader. More experienced decorators, or those working in specialist areas such as exterior painting or wallpaper hanging, will be at the higher end of this range.

Should I supply my own paint to save money?

You can supply your own paint, but discuss it with your decorator first. Professional decorators often have trade accounts giving them access to quality products at better prices than retail. If you have a specific brand or colour in mind, bring a tester pot or tin and discuss it before work starts.
