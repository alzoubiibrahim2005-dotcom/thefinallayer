Wallpaper Hanging in Manchester: Costs, Tips and What to Expect

Wallpaper can transform a room instantly, adding character and style to your Manchester or Lancashire home. But before you commit to a new look, it's important to understand the costs involved and what professional wallpaper hanging entails. This guide covers everything you need to know about getting wallpaper installed by qualified decorators in the North West.

Understanding Wallpaper Hanging Costs in Manchester

The cost of wallpaper hanging in Manchester varies depending on several factors, including room size, wall condition, wallpaper complexity and labour rates. On average, you can expect to pay between GBP150 and GBP400 for a standard bedroom, whilst larger living rooms or feature walls might cost GBP300 to GBP600 or more. These figures typically include labour only and don't include the cost of the wallpaper itself, which can range from budget-friendly options at GBP5 per roll to premium designer papers costing GBP30 or more.

Local Manchester decorators often charge hourly rates between GBP20 and GBP40 per hour, with most wallpaper hanging jobs taking between 6 and 16 hours depending on complexity. If your walls need preparation work such as filling, sanding or removing old wallpaper, this will add to the overall cost. It's always worth getting quotes from several painters and decorators in your area to compare pricing and ensure you're getting fair value for money.

Factors That Affect Your Wallpaper Hanging Quote

Several key factors influence the final cost of your wallpaper hanging project. Wall condition is crucial: smooth, well-prepared walls take less time and cost less to paper, whilst walls with cracks, damp patches or old adhesive require additional preparation and expense. The type of wallpaper you choose also matters significantly. Standard vinyl or paper wallpapers are straightforward to hang, but textured, heavily patterned or bespoke designs require greater skill and take longer, increasing labour costs.

The number of features in your room such as windows, doors, alcoves and cornicing also affects pricing. Each obstruction adds time to the job, as does pattern matching on large designs. Ceiling height is another consideration; rooms with high ceilings or sloped walls like loft spaces command premium rates. Additionally, if you're removing old wallpaper before hanging new, this preparation work typically adds GBP50 to GBP200 depending on how well the old covering adheres to the walls. What to Expect From Professional Wallpaper Hanging

Professional wallpaper hanging should follow a clear process from start to finish. Your decorator will first inspect the walls, discuss any preparation needed, and provide a detailed quote. Once you've agreed on pricing and timings, they'll prepare the walls properly, which may include filling holes, sanding rough patches, and applying primer if necessary. This preparation stage is vital because poor wall condition leads to wallpaper failure later on.

During hanging, skilled decorators will measure carefully, cut and paste each strip accurately, and smooth out air bubbles and wrinkles as they work. Pattern matching should be seamless, and edges should align perfectly at corners and around features like windows. A professional job should take between one and three days for an average room, depending on complexity. After completion, your decorator should trim excess wallpaper cleanly and ensure all edges are firmly adhered. They should also clear up thoroughly and dispose of waste properly. You'll typically be advised to avoid touching walls or opening windows for 24 hours to allow paste to dry fully.

Money-Saving Tips for Your Wallpaper Project

There are several ways to reduce wallpaper hanging costs without compromising on quality. First, choose simple, repeating patterns rather than complex designs with large pattern repeats, as these waste less material and hang faster, saving labour time. Solid colour wallpapers and small-pattern designs are particularly economical. Second, prepare your walls yourself before the decorator arrives if you're confident doing so. Removing old wallpaper, filling holes and light sanding can reduce preparation time significantly.

Third, consider having wallpaper hung in just your main living spaces rather than throughout your entire home, allowing you to spread costs over time. Feature walls are a trendy and cost-effective alternative to papering entire rooms. Fourth, bulk up your order with friends or family if you're planning multiple projects, as some decorators offer discounts for larger contracts. Finally, get at least three quotes to ensure competitive pricing in your area. Avoid choosing solely on price; ensure your chosen decorator is experienced, insured and has positive reviews from previous Manchester and Lancashire customers.

Choosing the Right Decorator in Manchester and Lancashire

Selecting a qualified, trustworthy decorator is essential for a successful wallpaper hanging project. Look for professionals with proven experience in wallpaper installation, evidenced by portfolios or customer testimonials. They should be fully insured, hold relevant qualifications, and be happy to provide references from recent clients. Check online reviews and ask whether they're members of professional bodies such as the Federation of Master Builders or similar trade associations.

When getting quotes, ensure they're detailed and specify exactly what's included. A good decorator will offer honest advice about wall preparation needs and realistic timescales. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does professional wallpaper hanging typically take?

Most wallpaper hanging jobs take between one and three days, depending on room size and complexity. A standard bedroom usually takes one day, whilst larger rooms or rooms with many features may take two to three days. Pattern matching and wall preparation can extend timescales.

Do I need to remove old wallpaper before hanging new?

In most cases yes, old wallpaper should be removed to ensure proper adhesion of new wallpaper and prevent future peeling. However, if the old wallpaper is in excellent condition and firmly adhered, some decorators may hang new paper over it. Your decorator will advise during their assessment.

What's the difference between paste and pre-pasted wallpaper?

Pre-pasted wallpaper has adhesive already applied and just needs soaking in water before hanging, making it quicker and cheaper to install. Standard paste wallpaper requires the decorator to apply paste to the back, offering more control and typically better results, especially for complex patterns.

How much does wallpaper cost to buy in Manchester?

Wallpaper prices vary widely, from budget options at GBP5 to GBP10 per roll to premium designer papers at GBP20 to GBP50 per roll or more. Most standard high street wallpapers range between GBP10 and GBP20 per roll. Always factor in purchasing slightly extra to account for pattern matching and potential mistakes.
