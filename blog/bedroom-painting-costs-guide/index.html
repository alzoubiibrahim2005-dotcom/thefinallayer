How Much Does It Cost to Paint a Bedroom in the UK? 2026 Guide

Planning to refresh your bedroom with a fresh coat of paint? Whether you're tackling a DIY project or hiring a professional decorator, understanding the true cost of painting a bedroom in 2026 is essential for budgeting. This guide breaks down labour costs, material expenses, and regional variations so you know exactly what to expect.

Average Bedroom Painting Costs in the UK

The cost to paint a bedroom in the UK typically ranges from GBP300 to GBP800 for professional work, depending on room size, condition, and complexity. For a standard 12x14 foot bedroom, most homeowners pay between GBP400 and GBP600 for labour and materials combined. Prices have increased modestly since 2025 due to inflation in paint products and labour rates, but remain competitive across most regions.

In Manchester and Lancashire, decorating costs tend to sit at the lower to mid-range of the national average. A local painter like Ibrahim Alzoubi at The Final Layer typically charges GBP150 to GBP250 per day, meaning a bedroom project lasting 1-2 days costs GBP150 to GBP500 in labour alone. Your exact quote depends on wall condition, number of coats needed, and whether you require additional prep work like filling, sanding, or primer application.

Breaking Down Labour and Material Costs

Labour typically accounts for 60-70% of your bedroom painting budget. Professional decorators in the UK charge between GBP20 and GBP40 per hour, with daily rates ranging from GBP120 to GBP300 depending on experience and location. A straightforward bedroom with good-condition walls usually takes one experienced decorator 1-2 days to complete, including preparation, painting, and cleanup.

Materials—including paint, primer, undercoat, brushes, rollers, tape, and drop sheets—usually cost between GBP80 and GBP150 for a bedroom project. Quality matters here; premium trade paint brands cost more but offer better coverage and durability. Budget GBP40-60 for a quality 10-litre tin of emulsion, plus GBP20-30 for primer if needed. Additional costs may include removing old wallpaper, repairing plaster, or applying multiple coats if you're changing from a dark colour to a light one.

Factors That Affect Your Final Quote

Several variables will influence what you actually pay. Room size is the most obvious—a large master bedroom costs more to paint than a small box room. Wall condition matters significantly; if your walls need extensive filling, sanding, and repair, expect to add GBP50-150 to labour costs. The number of coats required also impacts price; light colours over dark walls may need 2-3 coats, while painting light over light might need just one.

Ceiling height, trim complexity, and furniture removal all factor into the quote. High ceilings or ornate cornicing require more time and equipment (scaffolding or tall ladders), increasing costs by 20-30%. Whether you want your furniture moved, covered, or need the decorator to work around it will also affect the final price. In Manchester and Lancashire, seasonal demand influences rates slightly—summer months may see slightly higher prices due to increased demand, though professional decorators maintain consistent quality year-round.

Should You Paint It Yourself or Hire a Professional?

DIY painting can save GBP200-400 in labour costs, but requires time, skill, and the right equipment. If you're comfortable with a roller and brush, you'll spend mainly on materials—roughly GBP80-150. However, achieving a professional finish takes practice; uneven coverage, paint drips, and patchy coats are common mistakes that waste time and materials. Most people underestimate the preparation work required, which accounts for 40% of the job.

Hiring a professional decorator guarantees quality, speed, and proper preparation. In Manchester and Lancashire, experienced decorators like those at The Final Layer bring expertise in colour selection, surface preparation, and finishing techniques that result in a flawless, long-lasting finish. A professional completes the job in 1-2 days versus a weekend or longer for DIY, and they handle all the mess. For busy professionals or those wanting peace of mind, the GBP200-500 labour investment is typically worth the result.

Getting Accurate Quotes and Saving Money

Always request quotes from at least 2-3 local decorators before deciding. A good quote includes detailed labour costs, material breakdown, timeline, and any additional charges. Be wary of quotes significantly lower than the average—they may indicate rushed work or hidden costs. Request references or photos of previous bedrooms they've painted to assess quality.

To save money, prepare your room thoroughly before the decorator arrives—move furniture out, remove pictures and wall hangings, and clear the floor. Choosing a neutral, in-stock paint colour rather than bespoke mixes also reduces costs. If budget is tight, prioritise the walls and defer ceiling or trim painting to a later date. Contact local Manchester and Lancashire decorators directly for free quotes; many offer discounts for multiple rooms or refer customers to specialists like Ibrahim Alzoubi at The Final Layer for competitive rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to paint a bedroom?

A standard bedroom typically takes 1-2 days for a professional decorator, depending on wall condition and number of coats. Preparation (filling, sanding, protecting furniture) often takes longer than the actual painting. Complex projects with repairs or high ceilings may extend to 2-3 days.

What's included in a painter's quote?

A professional quote should include labour hours, paint and materials costs, preparation work, number of coats, timeline, and any extras like furniture moving or rubbish removal. It should be itemised so you understand exactly what you're paying for.

Can I negotiate bedroom painting costs?

You can discuss rates, but quality decorators won't significantly undercut their standard prices. Instead, ask about discounts for multiple rooms, off-season rates, or suggest materials you've already purchased. Getting 2-3 quotes gives you leverage to compare fair pricing.

Is it cheaper to paint in winter or summer?

Summer typically sees higher demand and slightly higher rates, but winter weather can slow drying times and affect paint application quality. Most decorators maintain consistent pricing year-round; focus on finding a reliable, experienced professional rather than chasing seasonal discounts.
